Objective:

Grasping and reaching movements of octopuses were important in the potential conceptualisation and design of an octopus-inspired soft robot. This motion had to be recreated for silicone rubber as the soft-robotic analogue, assuming embedded cables, or pneumatic actuation as the force generator.

Methodology

  • Divided the muscle into segments based on the muscular structure of an octopus tentacle.
  • Created impulse piecewise functions of normal force on each segment in ANSYS Mechanical, and simulated the FEA model using hyperelastic models for the silicone rubber's material properties.
  • Tracked the tip and bend point trajectory and velocity, to compare with an live octopus tentacle motion and optimise the trajectory.
  • Ran CFD simulations for this motion underwater both with and without fluid-structure interaction considerations.

Results

  • Bend-point and tip trajectories were in good alignment with literature results.
  • CFD simulations predicted vortex regions that influenced the reaching motion profile underwater and the energy expended.
