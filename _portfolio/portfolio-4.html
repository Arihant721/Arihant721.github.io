Objective:

An aircraft seat design was proposed, as an Indian alternative to imported aircraft seats for light aircraft, and needed to adhere to federal standards of static and dynamic testing before it was airworthy.

Methodology

  • Representative images below so as to not disclose some details.
  • Set-up and simulated a crash simulation model on Altair Radioss to recreate impact loadings of 9G and 16G forces onto the seat at specified angles
  • Created a mesh of sufficient resolution and balanced shell and 3D elements to optimise accuracy while keeping total runtime acceptable
  • Utilised multiple material tests for materials used (Aluminum alloys, steel, foam) to incorporate non-linear geometric, contact and material effects in the simulation for more accurate results.

Results

  • Identified local hotspots of stress, and presented findings to the team, for re-design and prototype manufacturing.
  • Explored design modifications based on these results.
  • Further modifications in arrays (staggered holes vs inline) also showed improvements.
